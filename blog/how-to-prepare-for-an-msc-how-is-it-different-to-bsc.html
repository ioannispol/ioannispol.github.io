How to prepare for an MSc. How is it different to BSc?

A guide to prospective students want to do an MSc at Newcastle University

The MSc is the standard Masters qualification for taught courses in Science and Technology subjects such as Marine Engineering and Naval Architecture. It stands for Magister Scientiae, which is Latin for ‚ÄòMaster of Science‚Äô. In the UK, an MSc is usually a one-year full-time course amd unlike an undergraduate degree (BSc), the teaching and assessment period runs for a full 12 months. You will have a summer holiday during your degree, but this is when you‚Äôll be expected to complete your dissertation (no one said postgraduate study would be easy!). In order to qualify as a full Masters programme, an MSc will conclude with an extended project and written dissertation which consist the majority of the required credits. You‚Äôll be assigned a supervisor, but will be independently responsible for researching your topic, assembling data and analysing your findings. To prepare for an MSc in Marine Technology have the following in mind:

  1. Check the University‚Äôs website. This is the best way to start preparing for your upcoming studies since it has many information about the course and gives the best idea what to expect during the studies. Getting in touch with someone for the university might be the best option when you feeling lost or need to ask something more specific. And the best of all is that the academic staff is on your side, they want you to succeed as much as you want to have a successful year.
